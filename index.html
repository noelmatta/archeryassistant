Bow Type

There are 3 kinds of bows:

  • Traditional

    Traditional bows are best used for recreational purposes and are perfect for archers who value minimalism. Usually made from wood, these bows do not need any extra accessories.

  • Olympic

    Olympic bows are commonly used in competitive shooting and are composed of high-quality materials. They also can be modified with additional equipment like stabilizers and clickers.

  • Compound

    Compound bows are designed for hunters and are outfitted with a variety of pulleys to improve the archer's stamina while shooting arrows at extremely heavy poundages for maximum penetrative power.

Shaft Material

Arrow shafts come in many kinds. Wood arrows are often used with traditional bows and bought in bulk as they are cheap, but prone to breakage. Carbon is light-weight, resulting in a fast and straight flight from bow to target, but with weaker penetration. Strong and flexible, aluminum arrows offer deep penetration with a slow and heavy arc path as a trade-off.

Poundage

Poundage, also known as Draw Weight, is how heavy the pull of the bowstring is when it is fully stretched to 28 inches.

If you are new to archery, it is important to use a lighter poundage (below #25) so you can concentrate on perfecting your form! You can always increase your poundage in the future.
